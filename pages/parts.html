PARTS

CPU

The Central Processing Unit is the brain of the computer, and the one who processes everything. So one of the main things you want to focus to get a fast PC is finding a "high" core count and frequency one. The higher the amount of cores, the more tasks it can handle at a time, and the higher the frequency, the faster it can handle those tasks. Note that while building a PC, higher/more expensive does not always mean better, so find one that suits your needs.

MOTHERBOARD

The Motherboard is the main PCB (Printed Circuit Board) and it's the responsible of making the connection between every component on the PC. They all have lots of features and differences between each other but one of the main things you will need to check before buying is the socket it supports. That way, you'll know if it's compatible with your CPU since they also support various sockets, mostly per generation that comes out.

RAM

The Random-access Memory (not to be confused with the storage) is a form of memory that will read and write any data needed by the CPU at any given time. It does this in an instant, but it will also not retain the data once the PC is turned off. They come in capacities ranging from 2gb all the way up to 64gb per module, and they go into the RAM slots of the motherboard so it depends on how many yours has. If you're going to use more than 1 module (always recommended), try to always pair them by the same capacity and model.

GPU

The one everyone talks about, the Graphics Card! It's main task is to process any visuals or "graphics" and outputs them into your display, in this case, a monitor. So, if you want to play the newest most graphically intense games, or work in a field that requires heavy renders, video editing, graphics design, you should opt for getting a more powerful one. As said before, pricier doesn't always mean better so get what suits your needs, that wat you don't blow your entire budget on this one!

STORAGE

Storage comes in different types, but the 3 main ones are: HDD (Hard Drive), SSD (Solid State Drive) and NVMe SSD (Non-Volatile Memory Express SSD). Although their names sound complicated, the main variation is in speed, going from slowest to fastest respectively. It's highly recommended to use NVMe SSDs nowadays since the prices aren't much higher and they offer way faster read and write speeds, making your operating system experience and file management a lot more enjoyable, but remember that your MOBO should have M.2 slots available.

PSU

Power Supply Units are what give energy to our PC and with all the specifications they have, it can be daunting to choose one, but it's usually recommended to buy one that has a higher wattage that what your PC will need, mainly for upgradeability and your components actually getting the power needed to function, and secondly that you buy one from a reputable brand like Corsair, Seasonic, beQuiet!. There's always the extra of buying one with higher efficiency (bronze, silver, gold, platinum).

COOLING

CPU coolers are as needed as any other component in the list since it generates lots of heat and that means less performance. The main thing is that you need to get one that's compatible with your CPU and MOBO's socket. There are also 2 main types of coolers, the Tower Coolers, and the AIO Waterloop Coolers (All-in-one) . The latter are better at cooling but they also have drawbacks like being more noisy, precier, and take up more space. As always, the more beefy the component, the more cooling it needs!

CASE

What is a PC without a cool way to display it? Not only that but cases also protect your components from excessive dust, and any other possible harm that could come from having them laying around. They have spaces for cable management, and fans that allow your PC to have even better cooling. They also serve as a way to customize the looks of your build a bit more by choosing its color, size (depending on the form factor - ATX, Micro ATX, Mini ATX), and if you want a side panel to look into it!

EXTRAS

You will probably come accross some more things that you'll need while building your rig like Thermal Paste, which is always needed for your CPU to transfer the heat correctly to its cooler! Be sure to also have a good voltage regulator since you may not want your PC die after a power outage. You may also need a Wi-Fi card if your MOBO doesn't come with one already. It's always good to have a few screwdrivers in hand too. And of course, don't forget the monitor, keyboard, mouse and a flash-drive with the operating system!
