HOW TO BUILD

Preparation

To prevent any accidents happening or components dying during the process, make sure to have enough space to lay out your components, preferably on a table. If you want to ground yourself to have an extra guarantee that you won't fry any component, you can connect your PSU to the wall (with its switch off) and touch the metal parts every once in a while. You'll also want any tool that may help you hold your screws, as well as any manuals you may need (specially Motherboard).

CPU Installation

With your motherboard on the table, find the CPU socket, lift the lever from it's lock, and pull it to take out the plastic cover. Note that with AMD's AM4 CPUs and earlier the pins are on the CPU so you need to be extra careful when placing it into the socket. Make sure you align the triangle on your CPU with the one on the socket, and carefully place it in. You won't need to apply any extra pressure since if you aligned the arrow, it will fit nicely. Check to see if it's correctly in place, drop the retaining bracket and pull the lever back into its lock. You may need to apply more force on the lever with newer 12th and 13th gen Intel CPUs.

RAM

If you have 2 modules in a 4 slots motherboard, check the MOBO's manual to know on which modules it will perform best. It'll usually be the far most slot and second slot from your CPU. You always want to skip one slot when using 2 DIMMs for it to run on dual-channel, giving better performance. Lower the latches and align the notch on your DIMMs with small bump on the slots. It will not need much force, so if it's struggling to go in, make sure to check if the notch is aligned properly. Once the latches click, you know the DIMM is sloted correctly, and if they didn't go fully up, you can nudge them to their closed position.

CPU Cooler

Some CPUs come with stock coolers, and if you're not planning on overclocking or stressing your processor too much they can be cheap option, though there are many other great options available if you're looking for more power or a quieter experience. Regardless of your choosing, they will have their own steps for the installation so make sure you check the manual! Remove the film on the bottom of the cooler, apply thermal paste to the top of the CPU and place the CPU cooler on top of the CPU, lining up the mounting holes on the cooler with the screws on the motherboard. Secure it to the motherboard using the screws provided with the cooler and don't forget to connect it to the 3 or 4 pin fan header.

Storage

If you're using an SSD or a HDD, attach the SATA cables to the storage devices and the motherboard, making sure to plug them into the appropriate SATA ports on the motherboard. SATA cables are typically labeled, so it should be easy to match them up with the correct ports. If it's an HDD you'll also need to connect a power cable from the power supply to the drive. Once your storage devices are connected, you can secure them in the appropriate drive bays in the case. Make sure the drives are securely fastened to prevent them from moving around during operation.

Storage (M.2 Drives)

If you're using an M.2 drive, you might not need any additional cables, as these drives typically connect directly to the motherboard. Locate the M.2 slot typically located near the CPU socket on the motherboard. It's a small slot with a connector at the end, and it might be labeled "M.2", "M", or "NVMe". If your M.2 slot has a cover, remove it by unscrewing the retaining screw and sliding the cover off. Insert the M.2 drive into the slot at a 30-degree angle, making sure to align the notches on the drive with the corresponding notches in the slot. Push down gently on the drive until it clicks into place. Secure the drive with the screw and put the cover back on.

Install the Motherboard

Once your motherboard is assembled, it's time to install it into the case. But first, don't forget to place your I/O shield into the case, it's the metal cover for the ports, and it comes with your MOBO. Locate the standoffs (small metal screws) that came with your motherboard, and screw the standoffs into the case at the appropriate locations, according to the motherboard layout. Gently place the motherboard onto the standoffs, lining up the ports and connectors with the corresponding openings in the case. Secure the motherboard to the standoffs with screws, making sure not to overtighten.

Power Supply

Typically, the PSU installation area is at the bottom of the case, though some cases might have it at the top or back. If your PSU is modular, connect all the cables needed before inserting it into the case. It should be installed with the fan facing whatever direction it can pull air from, up or down. Slide the PSU into the installation area and secure it to the case using the screws provided. Connect the 24-pin ATX cable to the motherboard's 24-pin power header, and the CPU power cable to the CPU power header on the motherboard. If your graphics card needs more power connect the PCIe cables to the graphics card's power connectors and connect any SATA cables to the power supply as well.Make sure to consult your motherboard and graphics card manuals to determine the correct headers and connectors.

Install the Graphics Card

If you plan on using a dedicated graphics card you'll need to install it into the appropriate PCIe slot on the motherboard (the long slots under the CPU socket). Here are the steps to follow: Locate the PCIe slot on the motherboard that corresponds to your graphics card (usually the top one), and remove any covers or brackets from the back of the case that might be blocking the way. Gently insert the graphics card into the slot, making sure to push down firmly until the card clicks into place. Secure the card to the case with screws, making sure not to overtighten and finally connect the PCIe cable from your PSU to your GPU.

Wi-Fi Card

A Wi-Fi card allows you to connect to wireless networks, providing a convenient way to access the internet without needing to connect your PC to a wired network. If your PC does not have a built-in Wi-Fi adapter, or if the built-in adapter is not working properly, you may need to install a Wi-Fi card to connect to a wireless network. When installing a PCIe card, carefully insert the card into an available PCIe slot on the motherboard (usually the one below the GPU's slot), aligning the notches on the card with the notches in the slot. Press down firmly until the card clicks into place. If you're installing a USB adapter, simply plug it into an available USB port on the PC.

Final Connections

If you're installing case fans, you'll need to connect them to the appropriate fan headers on the motherboard or a fan controller (if your case has one). Fans typically have a 3- or 4-pin connector, which should match the header on the motherboard or controller. Some fans might also have an RGB (or ARGB) connector, which can be used to control the fan's lighting effects. Also attach all the USB type conectors and the front-panel cables to their respective headers. Consult the fan and motherboard manuals to determine which headers to use and how to connect them. You may finally turn on your PC to test that it's working before you do any cable management. Remember to connect your HDMI cable to your GPU if you have one!

OS, Drivers and Updates

You'll need to download the operating system (OS) installation files from the manufacturer's website and create a bootable USB drive. This process may differ depending on the OS you are installing. Plug the drive onto the back of the PC and turn it on, it should boot into the OS installer. If your system doesn't access your NVMe drive, while the PC is turning on, open the BIOS with F12, DEL or the specified key your MOBO's manufacturer specifies and look for an option related to M.2 or NVMe, and make sure it's set to "Enabled". Save your changes and exit the BIOS, and your M.2 drive should now be recognized by the system. This may vary depending on the manufacturer. Once installed, don't forget to also check for any updates, and go to your manufacturers' websites to update any drivers. Congratulations! You're done.
